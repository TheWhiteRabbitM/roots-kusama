01 / Problem

The oldest anti-Sybil system
in human history

As AI blurs the boundary between human and machine, current Proof of Personhood solutions are technically sophisticated but culturally thin — they verify existence without verifying belonging.

In small Southern Italian villages, every family carries a 'u nomme 'e casa — a house name, passed down orally for centuries. Invisible to official registries. Incomprehensible to outsiders. Impossible for any AI to fake.

Pre-digital identity
House names are hyper-local, orally transmitted, semantically rich, socially validated — and they grow organically as families branch over generations.
"You cannot fake belonging to a place if you do not know who the people are and what they are called."
02 / Core Insight

A living social graph
rooted in oral tradition

Anti-Sybil
By Design
House names are the original distributed identity system. Community-validated, append-only, tamper-resistant. A proto-blockchain that predates the internet by 400 years.
Living Graph
Always Growing
When a family branch forms, a new nickname is born. Each new name creates a new node: roots_parent_token_id linking to its ancestral ROOTS token.
The Bridge
Off → On Chain
We encode centuries of oral trust onto Kusama — preserving the living, evolving nature of the graph. The community maintains it simply by living.
The ROOTS token is not a financial instrument. It is the on-chain equivalent of a community certificate — proof that a specific person belongs here, attested by their peers.
03 / Architecture

Two layers, one truth

Off-Chain Layer
Oral Collection Sessions
Informal gatherings at bars, homes, piazzas. Document 60+ house names with etymology, family attribution, village quarter, lineage.
The Book of Names
A printed archive — 30–50 pages, deposited with the municipality. Cultural artifact with standalone value.
The Lineage Graph
Visual and digital DAG of how names branch over time. The conceptual model for the on-chain structure.
On-Chain Layer — Kusama
Kusama Identity Pallet
house_name · village_quarter · attestation_hash · lineage_parent — no custom smart contract needed.
ROOTS Token (Soulbound)
Native assets pallet, transfer: None at protocol level. Metadata on IPFS, hash anchored on-chain. roots_parent_token_id creates the DAG.
OpenGov Referendum
Gated vote: only attested identities participate. Real community question, result presented to municipality.

07 / ROOTS NFT

A living portrait
of your place in the graph

Every attested family receives a generative NFT — a unique visual artwork procedurally rendered from the family's position in the lineage graph. No two are the same. And each one evolves over time.

Trunk width
Proportional to lineage depth — ancient families grow wider, more imposing.
Branch count
Equals the number of living child tokens. When a new house name is born, the parent NFT grows a new branch.
Leaf density & color
Grows with reputation score. Shifts from terracotta (new family) to deep amber (ancient lineage).
Animation speed
Slower, more majestic for high-reputation families. The tree breathes at the rhythm of its history.
The NFT is alive. When a child token is minted with this token as roots_parent_token_id, the artwork re-renders with a new branch. The NFT grows.
Important Disclaimer
ROOTS NFTs have zero monetary value. They cannot be traded, sold, or exchanged on any market. They are identity attestation records — the visual representation of a family's place in the community graph. Nothing more.

Vote Weighting
Logarithmic fairness
In the OpenGov experiment, votes are weighted:

weight = 1 + log₂(reputation / 50) Elder families with deep roots carry more weight — mirroring the actual social dynamics of the village — without silencing newer voices.
Reputation is the on-chain crystallization of something the village already knows: some families have been here longer, witnessed more, and carry more social trust. We are not inventing this hierarchy — we are making it legible.
